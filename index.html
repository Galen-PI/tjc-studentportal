How your progress score works

Your score runs from 0 to 100. A lower number means things are on track. A higher number means there are areas your coordinators want to help you with. It's not a grade — it's a tool to make sure you get support when you need it.

On Track (0–29) — You're making good progress. Keep it up.
Watch Closely (30–59) — Some areas to pay attention to. Your coordinator may reach out.
Needs Attention (60–100) — Your team wants to connect with you soon to help get things back on track.

The score looks at your academic pace, trade progress, hours logged, and whether you have an active intervention plan. It updates automatically as your data changes.
